Trader Mindset & Risk Principles

  • Capital preservation first: risk 1–2% of equity per trade. Focus on staying in the game.
  • Plan the trade, trade the plan: only act on predefined setups with entry, stop, and target.
  • Probability mindset: judge decisions by process quality, not individual outcomes.
  • Detach from PnL: avoid revenge trading, track emotional state in your journal.
  • Continuous improvement: review results weekly, adapt sizing to volatility and performance.

Daily Trading Framework

Pre-Market Routine

  1. Review macro news, crypto catalysts, and on-chain flows.
  2. Mark support/resistance, trend context, and liquidity pools on key charts.
  3. Update watchlist with bias (bullish, bearish, neutral) and planned setups.
  4. Check risk dashboard: open exposure, drawdown limits, volatility regime.
  5. Define execution plan: alerts, position sizing, and invalidation levels.

Post-Market Routine

  1. Log trades with screenshots, rationale, and emotions.
  2. Tag trades by strategy to measure edge quality.
  3. Update performance metrics and risk exposure.
  4. Identify mistakes or standout executions.
  5. Plan adjustments for tomorrow (e.g., reduce size, avoid certain sessions).

Core Trading Strategies

1. Trend Continuation (Break & Retest)

Market context: Strong directional move with higher highs/lows (bull) or lower lows/highs (bear).

Execution: Enter on retest of prior structure or 21EMA with confirmation (volume spike, momentum oscillator).

Risk: Stop beyond invalidation structure; target 2R–3R or trail using swing highs/lows.

Notes: Avoid during consolidations; track success rate vs. market regime.

2. Range Reversion

Market context: Sideways range with clear boundaries and volume tapering.

Execution: Fade moves into range extremes using confirmation (rejection wick, divergence).

Risk: Stop outside the range; scale out near midline and opposite boundary.

Notes: Cancel trade if breakout retest holds. Avoid trading just before major news.

3. Breakout Momentum

Market context: Consolidation leading to volatility contraction (e.g., Bollinger squeeze).

Execution: Enter on confirmed breakout with volume surge or closing basis; add on pullbacks.

Risk: Initial stop below breakout level, trail aggressively to protect gains.

Notes: Track win rate by session time; avoid chasing extended breakouts.

4. News/Event Reaction

Market context: Scheduled events (FOMC, CPI, exchange listings) create volatility spikes.

Execution: Wait for initial impulse to settle, then trade pullback toward key levels with tight risk.

Risk: Use reduced size; slippage is high. Cancel trade if spread widens drastically.

Notes: Log event specifics to refine filters (e.g., only trade positive surprises).

Performance Metrics Dashboard

Key Ratios

  • Win Rate
  • Average R Multiple
  • Expectancy (R)
  • Max Drawdown
  • Sharpe / Sortino (weekly)

Risk Controls

  • Daily Loss Limit: -3R
  • Weekly Loss Limit: -7R
  • Max Concurrent Positions: 3
  • Leverage Cap: 3x average
  • Capital at Risk < 6% total

Improvement Focus

  • Tag-based performance heatmap
  • Session performance (Asia, EU, US)
  • Mistake log vs. rule violations
  • Execution grade (A/B/C)
  • Mental state correlations

Weekly Review Checklist

  1. Summarize weekly PnL and expectancy per strategy.
  2. Identify best and worst trade — focus on process, not outcome.
  3. Review screenshots and note recurring patterns.
  4. Adjust risk if drawdown exceeds thresholds.
  5. Set goals for next week (e.g., reduce overtrading, execute alerts faster).
